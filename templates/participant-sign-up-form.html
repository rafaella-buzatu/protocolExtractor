The process of transforming undifferentiated human induced pluripotent stem (iPS) cells and embryonic stem (ES) cells into medically useful specialized cell types is called differentiation, and each cell type requires a dedicated "protocol". Thousands of scientists work worldwide and publish differentiation protocols in numerous formatting styles. The absence of a harmonized database is a major bottleneck for regenerative medicine because it makes protocol development and replication very difficult.
